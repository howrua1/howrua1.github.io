If you’re looking for a natural hairline without having to deal with your natural hair, a frontal is definitely the answer. Installing a frontal may seem like a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, you’ll be sure to have flawless results in no time.

Pain Points of Installing a Frontal

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to installing a frontal is making sure it lays flat and looks natural. Another pain point is having to deal with the maintenance of the frontal, such as washing and styling.

How to Install a Frontal

Begin by making sure your hair is thoroughly washed and dried. Braid your natural hair down and secure it with a wig cap. Place the frontal on your head to determine where it should be placed. Cut off any excess lace. Apply adhesive to the perimeter of the frontal and on your hairline. Place the frontal onto your head and use a blow dryer to set it in place. Style as desired.

Main Points of Installing a Frontal:

  • Wash and dry your hair
  • Braid down your natural hair and use a wig cap
  • Apply adhesive to the perimeter of the frontal and hairline
  • Use a blow dryer to set it in place

How to Style a Frontal

Personal Experience: I like to style my frontal with some loose curls to add some volume. To do this, I would use a curling iron or hot rollers. Before styling, I always make sure to use a heat protectant to prevent any damage to the hair.

When styling a frontal, it’s important to use a low heat setting and avoid applying heat directly to the lace. This can cause the lace to melt and damage the hair. After styling, it’s important to let the hair cool before touching it to prevent the curls from falling out.

How to Maintain a Frontal

Personal Experience: I like to wash my frontal every 2-3 weeks to keep it clean and fresh. Before washing, I always make sure to use a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup. After washing, I use a leave-in conditioner and let the hair air dry.

When washing a frontal, it’s important to be gentle and avoid rubbing or tangling the hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a wide-tooth comb to detangle. After washing, make sure to let the hair air dry completely before applying heat.

How to Remove a Frontal

When it’s time to remove your frontal, begin by using a glue solvent on the perimeter of the frontal. Gently lift the lace and apply more glue solvent. Use a cotton swab to remove any excess adhesive. Once the frontal is removed, gently wash your natural hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and deep condition.

Removing a Frontal:

  • Use a glue solvent on the perimeter of the frontal
  • Gently lift the lace and apply more glue solvent
  • Use a cotton swab to remove any excess adhesive
  • Wash natural hair with sulfate-free shampoo and deep condition

Question and Answer

Q: How long does a frontal last?

A: A frontal can last up to 6 weeks with proper maintenance.

Q: Can I swim with a frontal installed?

A: It’s best to avoid swimming with a frontal installed, as the water can cause the adhesive to loosen and the hair to tangle.

Q: Can I sleep with a frontal installed?

A: It’s not recommended to sleep with a frontal installed, as it can cause the hair to tangle and the adhesive to loosen.

Q: Can I color my frontal?

A: Yes, you can color your frontal. It’s best to go to a professional stylist to avoid damaging the hair.

Conclusion of How to Install a Frontal

Installing a frontal may seem intimidating, but with these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to achieve flawless results. Remember to take care of your frontal with proper maintenance and styling to ensure it lasts as long as possible.
