As trivial as it may seem, installing a toilet paper holder is an important aspect of any bathroom renovation. It is an easy task that can be done by anyone, but it is essential to do it right to avoid any future problems. In this article, we will guide you through the process of how to install a toilet paper holder, step by step.

The Pain Points of Installing a Toilet Paper Holder

Mounting the toilet paper holder might seem like a straightforward process, but it can become frustrating if you are not aware of some of the common mistakes people make. One of the most common issues is placing the holder too far away from the toilet, making it difficult to reach, or not accounting for the roll of toilet paper’s weight, causing it to keep falling off. Both issues can be a significant inconvenience in the long run.

How to Install a Toilet Paper Holder

To install the toilet paper holder, you will need a drill, screwdriver, measuring tape, level, and a pencil. First, measure the distance between the two mounting brackets on the holder itself. Then, mark the same distance on the wall using a pencil or marking tool. Using a level, ensure that the markings are straight. Afterward, drill holes where you marked and insert screw anchors. Align the mounting brackets with the holes and screw them into place. Finally, attach the toilet paper holder to the mounting brackets.

Summary of How to Install a Toilet Paper Holder

Installing a toilet paper holder involves measuring the distance between mounting brackets, marking it on the wall, drilling holes, inserting screw anchors, attaching the mounting brackets, and finally, attaching the holder itself. It is essential to perform these steps effectively to avoid any future problems.

How to Install a Toilet Paper Holder with a Personal Experience

During our bathroom renovation, we had to install a toilet paper holder. While it seemed like a trivial task, it took us longer than expected since we did not use a level, resulting in an uneven holder. To fix our mistake, we had to redo the entire process, which was frustrating. We learned that using a level is crucial to ensuring a straight holder.

Mistakes to Avoid When Installing a Toilet Paper Holder

A common mistake people make when installing a toilet paper holder is not using a level. This results in a crooked holder, making it difficult to use. Another mistake is placing the holder too far from the toilet, making it hard to reach, or not accounting for the weight of the toilet paper roll.

How to Install a Toilet Paper Holder Properly

It’s essential to measure and mark the distance between the mounting brackets, use a level to ensure that the markings are straight, drill straight and even holes, insert screw anchors, and securely attach the mounting brackets. Make sure the toilet paper holder is level by using a level before fixing it to the brackets. This ensures that the holder is secure and easy to use.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I install a toilet paper holder on tiles?

A: Yes, you can install a toilet paper holder on tiles. You will need a drill with a masonry bit to drill holes on the tile and then follow the regular installation process.

Q: Can I install a toilet paper holder without a drill?

A: It’s not advisable to install a toilet paper holder without a drill. Drilling is necessary to make clean and precise holes to ensure that the holder is secure.

Q: How far away from the toilet should the holder be?

A: The holder should be about 8-12 inches away from the toilet and reachable for the user.

Q: Can I install a toilet paper holder without screw anchors?

A: It’s not recommendable to install a toilet paper holder without screw anchors as they ensure that the screws are tightly secured in place. Screw anchors provide a strong hold, especially in drywall installation.

Conclusion of How to Install Toilet Paper Holder

Installing a toilet paper holder might seem easy, but it’s crucial to perform it effectively. It involves measuring and marking the distance between mounting brackets, using a level, drilling holes, inserting screw anchors, and securing the mounting brackets and holder. By following these steps, you can ensure that your holder is securely in place and easy to use for everyone. Avoid common mistakes like placing the holder too far from the toilet or not using a level to have a seamless installation process.
