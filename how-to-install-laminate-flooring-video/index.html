Tools You’ll Need for Installing Laminate Flooring

Before you start installing laminate flooring, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. Here are some of the key items you’ll need:

  • Laminate flooring planks
  • Underlayment
  • Tape measure
  • Saw
  • Circular saw or chop saw
  • Jigsaw
  • Utility knife
  • Tapping block
  • Pry bar
  • Hammer
  • Pencil or chalk

Preparing the Subfloor

The first step in installing laminate flooring is to prepare the subfloor. This involves making sure the surface is clean and level, as well as removing any old flooring that might be in the way. You’ll also need to lay a moisture barrier in areas like basements or bathrooms to prevent water damage.

How to Lay Underlayment

After preparing the subfloor, the next step is to lay the underlayment. This material will protect your floors from moisture and provide a stable base for the planks. Start by rolling out your underlayment in strips, overlapping each strip by about 2 inches. Cut the strips to fit around obstacles like door frames and vents.

How to Install Laminate Flooring Planks

Finally, it’s time to install the laminate flooring planks. Start by cutting your planks to size, leaving a gap around the edges for expansion. Use a tapping block and pry bar to fit the planks together snugly. Take your time and make sure each plank is level as you go.

Question and Answer

What’s the Best Way to Cut Laminate Flooring Planks?

The best way to cut laminate flooring planks is with a saw. A circular saw or chop saw will do the job quickly and easily. For more detailed cuts, a jigsaw can also be used.

Do I Need Underlayment for Laminate Flooring?

Yes, it’s recommended to use underlayment when installing laminate flooring. This will protect your floors from moisture and provide a stable base for the planks.

How Long Does it Take to Install Laminate Flooring?
