How to Install a LED Light Bar and What to Keep in Mind

Before diving into the how-to guide, let me share my personal experience of installing a LED light bar. Wiring of Light Bar to the Vehicle

Once you have secured the light bar, it’s time to connect the wiring of the light bar to your vehicle’s battery. It’s best to have a pre-existing wiring harness for easy and quick installation. You can also buy a harness separately if your light bar doesn’t come with one. Make sure that you connect with the right wires, whether you’re connecting to the high beam or the battery directly.

Wire Routing and Switch Installation

To make the installation look more professional, routing the wires correctly is vital. You can hide the wires along the frame and secure them with the zip ties for better looks. After that, a switch is needed to turn the light bar on and off. You can use the existing slots present on your switches board.

Testing the Light bar

Now you’re almost done, and it’s time to test the lightbar if it’s working correctly. Turn on the vehicle and flip the switch. You should see the LED light up. And if everything seems to work fine, then congratulations, you have successfully installed the LED light bar.

Things to Keep in Mind After Installation

The first and foremost thing you need to keep in mind is to ensure that your light bar stays clean. Dirt and mud can build up on the surface of the light and may eventually cause damage to the lenses. Additionally, you need to check if the wire connections are secure and ensure if the switch is working correctly.

Question and Answer

Q1. Should I install the light bar myself or hire a professional?

A: If you’re familiar with working with cars, then you can do the installation yourself. However, if you’re not confident or lack the required knowledge, it’s best to hire a professional installer.

Q2. What are the legal issues regarding the light bar installation?

A: Different states have different laws regarding light bar installation. Therefore, it’s better to check your state’s laws and regulations related to adding an auxiliary light.

Q3. Can I install a light bar on any vehicle?

A: Yes, you can install a light bar on any vehicle, but make sure that you choose a size that is proportionate to your car.

Q4. Do LED light bars reduce fuel consumption, or do they consume more battery?

A: When compared to traditional halogen lights, LED lights use less power, which ultimately means that they consume less fuel. Additionally, they also have a longer lifespan and lower maintenance cost.
