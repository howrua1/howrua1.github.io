If you’re planning on installing new flooring in your home, it’s essential to have a strong and stable subfloor as a foundation. A properly installed subfloor can prevent damage to your new floors, ensure smooth installation, and improve the overall quality and durability of your floors. In this article, we’ll take you through the steps of how to install subfloor.

Problems That Can Arise Without Proper Subfloor Installation

Without a good subfloor, your new flooring can develop many issues, including squeaky, bouncy, or uneven floors. These issues can cause damage to your new floors, creating high repair costs, and potentially decreasing the value of your home. Additionally, improper subfloor installation can lead to moisture buildup and mold growth, which can compromise the quality of your flooring and create health hazards in your home.

Answering How to Install Subfloor

The first step in installing subfloor is measuring the area’s dimensions and checking for level and flatness. Then you’ll need to choose the right materials for your subfloor, typically plywood or OSB boards, cut them to size and lay them down evenly. The boards should be securely fastened and glued according to the manufacturer’s instructions.

Summary of How to Install Subfloor

Installing a subfloor requires precise measuring, selecting the right materials, laying them down evenly, and securely fastening them according to the manufacturer’s instructions. Make sure the subfloor is level, flat and has a moisture barrier to protect against moisture buildup and mold growth.

How to Install Subfloor: Plywood Subfloor Installation

When installing subfloor, we recommend using plywood. Plywood subfloors provide a strong and stable foundation for your new floors, ensuring durability and longevity. Before installation, remove any existing flooring and check for level and flatness.

Begin by measuring and cutting the plywood pieces to size, keeping in mind any necessary cuts for vents or obstacles in the room. Lay the plywood pieces down, making sure they fit together snugly, and fasten them securely to the floor joists with screws, every 6 to 8 inches around the edges, and every 12 inches throughout the rest of the subfloor. It’s essential to follow manufacturer instructions for spacing and screw sizes. Lastly, fill any gaps with wood filler before moving onto your new flooring installation.

How to Install Subfloor: Concrete Subfloor Installation

Concrete subfloors provide an excellent foundation for your new floors, but they require slightly different installation techniques than plywood subfloors. Before installation, ensure that the concrete is clean, dry, and level. Also, make sure to lay down a vapor barrier to prevent moisture buildup.

To begin installation, lay down a layer of plywood over the concrete, fastened securely using a construction adhesive. This step helps provide an even surface, prevents moisture buildup, and insulates against cold concrete floors. Next, add a layer of subfloor panels, such as OSB boards or plywood sheets over the plywood layer and fasten them firmly, ensuring gaps are minimised. Before moving onto flooring installation, create a flat, clean, and level surface by sanding or using floor levelling compound as needed.

How to Install Subfloor: Soundproof Subfloors

If you’re looking to install a subfloor that provides sound isolation in multi-story homes, consider soundproof subfloors. These subfloors create a sound barrier between floors, helping to reduce noise levels in different rooms or apartments. These subfloors are installed similarly to standard plywood or OSB subfloors, with the addition of sound barriers in between layers. Ensure you check with a professional to select the right subflooring for your specific needs.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need to use adhesive when installing the subfloor?

A: Yes, it’s necessary to use a construction adhesive to secure the subfloor because it provides additional stability, reduces squeaking, and prevents moisture buildup.

Q: Can I install subfloor over old flooring?

A: It’s not recommended to install subfloor over old flooring. You’ll have to remove the existing flooring, check for levelness, and install the subfloor directly on the floor joists to ensure proper stability and durability.

Q: How thick should subfloor be?

A: Plywood subfloors should be at least 5/8" thick, while OSB subfloors should be at least 3/4" thick. Thicker subfloors provide extra durability and help reduce squeaking and bouncing.

Q: Can subfloor be installed on concrete subfloor?

A: Yes, subfloor can be installed over a concrete subfloor. Ensure that the concrete subfloor is level, flat, and smooth, add a vapor barrier, lay down a layer of plywood, and then add the subfloor panels of your choice.

Conclusion of How to Install Subfloor

Properly installing subfloor is essential to ensure the durability and quality of your new flooring. Remember to measure and level the area, select the right materials, securely fasten the boards, and fill any gaps before moving onto your new flooring installation. If you follow these steps, you’ll be able to enjoy your new floors without worrying about squeaks, bounces, or unevenness.
