Loading up your kitchen with brand new cabinets is a great way to transform the look and feel of the space entirely.

When it comes to upgrading the cabinetry, many homeowners focus solely on the aesthetics of the cabinets, like the style, color, and finish. But what about nitty-gritty details like installing cabinet handles?

Installing cabinet handles can be a daunting task but it is essential to achieve a cohesive look and feel in your kitchen. Here’s our guide on how to install cabinet handles:

First and foremost, the most critical thing when installing cabinet handles is to measure precisely. Do not eyeball your measurements or rely on guess-work as it may lead to uneven placement. After measuring, lay out a template on your cabinet door where you want the handles to be installed, then mark where the holes ought to be with a pencil.

Our personal experience with how to install cabinet handles

When we were renovating our kitchen and installing the cupboards, we got stuck when it came to putting on the handles. We did not know which tools to use and found that we were drilling the holes in the wrong places, making it challenging to open the cabinet doors smoothly. We discovered that using a template was incredibly helpful, and measuring twice helped us drill holes once.

Picking the right hardware

Choosing the hardware for your kitchen cabinetry is also essential and can be an overwhelming task. The market is flooded with all sorts of knobs and handles made from different materials, types, sizes, and designs. It’s crucial to think beyond aesthetics and decide on hardware that is comfortable to grip and fits your style of living.

Steps to install cabinet handles

1. Measure the drilling holes: Use a ruler or a tape measure to determine the distance from the center of one hole to the center of the other hole on each handle.

2. Create a template: Use a template to place the handles on the cabinet doors and drawers uniformly.

3. Drill Holes: Using a drill bit, carefully drill the holes in the spots marked on the template, ensuring that you drill straight and that the depth of the drill bit is must not be longer than the screws supplied with the handles.

Benefits of updating your cabinet handles

Updating your cabinet handles is a quick-and-easy way to upgrade your kitchen without breaking the bank. Swapping out an old handle for a new one can add personality and style to the room. Moreover, it the cheapest way to give your kitchen a refreshing look.

Question and Answer

Q1. What tools will I need to install cabinet handles?

A1. You will need a power drill, drill bits, measuring tape, and a handle template.

Q2. What is the standard height to install cabinet handles?

A2. The standard height to install cabinet handles is that they should be installed at a height of 2.5" to 3" from the bottom edge of the cabinet door.

Q3. How do I know if I need handles with longer screws?

A3. It’s essential to measure before ordering. The thickness of your door will determine if you will need handles with longer screws.

Q4. Can I install cabinet handles myself, or should I hire a professional?

A4. Cabinet handles installation is relatively easy and can be done by any handy homeowner. Follow the steps provided, and you will have no trouble installing them yourself. If rather you not, then you may hire professionals who may get the job done even faster.

Conclusion of how to install cabinet handles

Installing cabinet handles is a perfect way to bring life to your kitchen. It’s essential to take the extra time to measure accurately to ensure uniform placement. With our guide, you can do it yourself and upgrade your kitchen cabinetry the right way.
